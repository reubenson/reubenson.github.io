Airs is a collection of poems accompanied by sound recordings of the quiet and teeming air in two parts which you can navigate by clicking on the left and right sides of the page/screen

dawn comes whispering

down

from the trees

exhaling under thin blue expanse

earth fuming petrichor

rises &

wafts

holding diaphragm, or

adorning diaresis

you bask

and tilt under the rolling sky

gathering upon

the pure albedo of glaciers

you walk so

lightly

you carry the wind under your feet

rain spreads water

to hair

coalesces to translucent sheet

a cone of dewy plastic

enclasped green stems hold

their bloom

as they wind their way

backwards

and back

winds rise in the north

when free

of confining fjords

acquiring heat and moisture

frictional contrasts

that disconnect the flow

cold air is

denser

and it sinks

listlessly sounding

up there

or down here

in summer it is most persistent

a blanket of thick moisture-rich clouds

warmed by descent

from the crest

forcing lake waves into the mouths of rivers

as if teeming

multiplying the air into air

and more air

as if speeding

crashing vapors into abstraction

and other forms of statecraft

stunned into stillness

when swirling mass cracks

in half under thin white

tines of forking light

pale red and

shearing

seraphim

of total caesura

without suture or

self-cure

an unappointed storm-cloud

from one or another

of the neighboring centuries

a clatter of saucer

and cup appears

the receding tide of foam

to soft brown lace

yawning for air

the dust in your brain

settles like swept dunes

over epochs of stone

a ghost paces

still and moving lines

tracing breath


a window

you open

and it is all around you

you throw everything the wind

that you want to float on

like tapered wood a

curling arc under voices

of the night sky

the aura of night itself

frogpondering

singing from

the vast library of air

rippling air

dances ear

every thing

is quieter in the shadow

of a leaf

to the wind three sheets

The second part of Airs is a visually animated poem of infinite duration. It begins with a rectangular arrangement of the letter 'x' repeated over and over, creating a typographic grid resembling the screen of a window in your home that keeps bugs from getting in while you have the window open. Audio data from the recordings of wind is processed and converted to a moving image, and then overlaid on top of the typographic grid. Various CSS filters are then applied to these two visual elements, producing an animation that is expressive of what a concrete poem would look like if turned into a screensaver.
