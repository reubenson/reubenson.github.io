rain

spreads water to hair

coalesces

to translucent sheet

a cone

of dewy plastic


enclosed,

under wrapped arms

green stems hold

their bloom

as they wind their way

backwards


and backwards

followed closely by

another

they wind their way

home

everything you already know

dawn comes whispering

streaming

down

from the trees under tilting

sky of cerulean perfume


earth fuming petrichor

rises & wafts

you walk so

lightly

you carry the wind

under your feet

a certain weight

tapered wood

of curling arc under voices

of the night sky


you toss everything into the wind

that you want to float on

back

instead of being a snow-eater

winds rise in the north

when free

of confining fjords


acquire heat and moisture

frictional contrasts

that disconnect the flow

oscillations

the dust in your brain

settles like swept dunes

under a blanket of silence


a ghost paces

still and moving lines

tracing every breath


a window

you open

and it is all around you

albedo of glaciers

In summer it is most persistent

persistent

blanket of thick moisture-rich clouds

Warmed by descent

from the crest

forcing lake waves into the mouths of rivers


A clatter of saucer

and cup appears

the receding tide of foam

to soft brown lace

yawning for air

rustle

colder air is

denser

and it sinks.

holding diaphragm, or diaresis


the sound, that rustles

every thing

is quieter in the shadow

of a leaf

night

the aura of night itself

a chorus of frogs

singing from

the vast library of air

to the wind

three sheets
