After Orpheus

Media: ceramics, c-stand, steel wire, ceramics | Year: 2025
Mixed media sculpture

After Orpheus is a mixed media sound sculpture. It combines steel piano wire with a bell-shaped ceramic vessel that serves to amplify the resonance of steel piano wire.

The vessel sits on top of a small stack of bricks, which weigh down and conceal an anchoring base plate that holds tension across the piano wire. The construction of this sculpture is inspired by the millenia-old tradition of aeolian instruments activated by the wind, but in this case the wind is replaced by electromagnetic activation (via e-bow).

This sculpture is the first iteration of what I envision as a template for further experimentation, in which the latent acoustics of ceramic vessels are activated through various means.

A pile of bricks, a ceramic bell, and a c-stand frame the physical tension of a long steel wire that quietly sings. With a cup held up against the wire, you can almost hear a willing of stones to dance once more, the ghost of the lyre of Orpheus.
